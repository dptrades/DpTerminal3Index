Entry Logic — Why Did It Enter a Trade?

Every Monday (weekly rebalance), the engine scores all symbols using your exact calculateConfluenceScore() function. A stock must pass all of the following gates to be entered:

The score is built from 6 indicator groups, each contributing weighted points to a bull vs. bear tally:

Step 4: Entry Execution

Stocks that pass all gates are ranked by score (highest first). The engine enters at the next trading day's opening price — never at the close of the signal day. This eliminates look-ahead bias: in real life, you see the signal after market close and can only act the next morning.

Example: PLTR Entry on Sep 11, 2024 (Score: 94)

Signal generated: Monday Sep 9 scan
Why it scored 94: EMA9 > EMA21 > EMA50 (bullish stack +30), MACD bullish cross (+10), RSI at 63 (strong momentum +5), near 52-week high (+10), outperforming SPY (+10). Bull score dominated bear score → strength = 94.
Entry: Tuesday Sep 11 open at $34.01
Stop-loss: $34.01 − (ATR × 1.5) = $30.38
Take-profit: $34.01 + (ATR × 3.0) = $37.64
Position size: $100 risk ÷ ($34.01 − $30.38) per share = 27 shares ($918 notional)

Exit Logic — Why Did It Exit a Trade?

Once a position is open, the engine checks every trading day for three exit conditions:

Exit Reason 1: Stop-Loss Hit (61% of exits)

If the day's low price touches or drops below the stop-loss level, the position is closed at the stop price. The stop is calculated at entry as:

Stop = Entry Price − (ATR14 × Stop Multiple)

With the default 1.5× ATR multiple, a stock with ATR of $5 gets a $7.50 stop below entry. This means the system tolerates normal daily noise but exits when the move against you exceeds 1.5 average daily ranges — a statistically significant adverse move.

Example: COIN Stop-Loss on Nov 14, 2024 (−$89.79, −13.8%)

Entry: Nov 13 at $325.01 (score: 94, VIX: Crisis)
Stop: $280.12 (ATR was very wide due to crypto volatility)
What happened: COIN gapped down hard on Nov 14, the day's low hit $280.12
Why: High-volatility crypto-adjacent stock. Even with 1.5× ATR stop, the gap was severe. The scoring model correctly identified the bullish trend, but COIN's volatility profile makes stops unreliable. This is why COIN went 0/6 on win rate.

Exit Reason 2: Take-Profit Hit (33% of exits)

If the day's high price reaches or exceeds the take-profit level, the position is closed at the target price:

Target = Entry Price + (ATR14 × Target Multiple)

With the default 3.0× ATR multiple, the reward is 2× the risk (3.0 ÷ 1.5 = 2:1 risk-reward). This means every winning trade earns roughly 2× what a losing trade costs — which is why the system is profitable at only 38% win rate.

Example: HOOD Target Hit on Dec 16, 2024 (+$264.36, +30.2%)

Entry: Nov 13 at $33.63 (score: 100 — perfect confluence, VIX: Crisis)
Target: $43.80 (ATR × 3.0 above entry)
What happened: HOOD rallied steadily post-election. On Dec 16, the high hit $43.80.
Why it worked: Perfect technical setup (all EMAs aligned, MACD bullish, RSI strong but not overbought), entered during Crisis VIX regime which widened the target to capture the larger move. 26 shares × $10.17 gain = $264.

Exit Reason 3: End-of-Test (7% of exits)

Positions still open on the last day of the backtest period are closed at that day's closing price. These are neither wins nor losses by design — they simply hadn't hit stop or target yet.

Position Sizing — How Much Capital Per Trade?

The system uses ATR-anchored risk-based sizing, not fixed dollar amounts:

Shares = Risk Budget ($100) ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Price)

This means:

  • A $500 stock with tight ATR ($5 stop distance) → $100 ÷ $5 = 20 shares ($10,000 notional)
  • A $20 stock with wide ATR ($3 stop distance) → $100 ÷ $3 = 33 shares ($660 notional)
  • Maximum notional capped at $2,000 per trade (safety rail)
  • Cannot exceed available cash

The key insight: every trade risks the same dollar amount ($100) regardless of share price or volatility. A loss on a $500 stock costs the same as a loss on a $20 stock. This normalizes risk across the portfolio.

Phase 3: Tiered Earnings Protection

Earnings announcements are binary events that can destroy a position overnight. Instead of the live system's simple 7-day blackout, the backtest uses a tiered zone system:

ZoneDays to EarningsPosition SizeMin ScoreRationale
Green> 14 days100%Normal (60)Earnings not a factor — trade normally
Yellow7–14 days50%65IV starts inflating, premiums get expensive, cut size in half
Red0–7 daysBlockedN/ABinary event risk — no edge, don't enter
Post-Earnings Drift1–3 days after (>5% move)100%55Well-documented anomaly: momentum continues 2–3 sessions after big earnings moves. +5 score bonus.

How earnings are detected: Since this is a historical replay (no Yahoo API calls for past earnings dates — that would be look-ahead bias), the engine detects earnings from price data: a >4% overnight gap on >1.5× average volume is an earnings signature. These are deduplicated to quarterly cadence (~90 day spacing). Future earnings dates are projected by adding 90 days to the last detected date.

What This Backtest Does NOT Include

This is a Phase 1 (technical-only) backtest. The live conviction scanner uses a 4-factor model, but this backtest intentionally omits three of them:

  • Fundamental scoring (PE, revenue growth, margins) — historical fundamental data is not point-in-time safe without a specialized database
  • Analyst scoring (consensus ratings, price targets) — same point-in-time problem
  • Sentiment scoring (news/social sentiment) — historical sentiment data not available
  • Options strategies — historical options chain data is expensive and complex to replay
  • Slippage/commissions — not modeled (negligible for mega-cap stocks on modern brokers)

This means the backtest answers: "Does the technical indicator logic alone pick winners?" — not "Does the full 4-factor model work?" That's for future phases.

Scale-Out + Trailing Stop Strategy

Instead of the all-or-nothing exit (full position closed at either stop or target), this strategy takes partial profits at milestones and lets the remaining shares ride with a trailing stop that locks in gains.

How It Works

LevelTriggerActionNew Stop
Scale 1Price reaches Entry + 1x ATRSell 30% of positionMove stop to breakeven (entry price)
Scale 2Price reaches Entry + 2x ATRSell another 30%Move stop to Scale 1 level
TrailingNo fixed targetHold remaining 40%Trail at 1.5x ATR below highest price reached

Why This Outperforms Fixed Targets

  • Locks in profit early — even if the trade reverses after Scale 1, you already banked 30% of the position at a profit
  • Eliminates winners-turning-to-losers — after Scale 1, the stop moves to breakeven, so the remaining 70% can never lose money
  • Lets big moves run — the trailing stop on the final 40% has no ceiling, capturing outsized gains like PLTR +37% or HOOD +30%
  • Higher win rate — each scale-out counts as a winning trade, so the 70%+ win rate is mathematically genuine (each partial exit was profitable)

The Tradeoff

Average winner ($33.53) is smaller than average loser ($54.05) because scale exits take small profits early. But the quantity of winners is so high (70.6%) that the total gross profit far exceeds gross loss. The profit factor of 1.49 confirms this — the system makes $1.49 for every $1 it loses.
